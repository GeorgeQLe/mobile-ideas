# Ro-Style Clone Spec

> Metadata
> - Inspiration app: Ro
> - Category: Direct-to-consumer telehealth
> - Readiness status: Implementation-ready for a lawful public-source V1 clone as of 2026-05-01.
> - Verification basis: exact public App Store listing, Ro support/privacy/terms pages, care-path pages, weight-loss pages, and public online-care disclosures.
> - Manual verification blockers: state-by-state clinician licensure, care-path protocols, weight-loss medication eligibility, lab-order integrations, and subscription fulfillment require hands-on verification with a licensed clinical organization.
> - Legal scope: lawful functional parity only; original code, brand, copy, and clinical/pharmacy/lab partnerships. Operator is not a clinical provider; no medical advice.

## Overview

Build an original direct-to-consumer telehealth platform inspired by Ro: condition-focused care paths (weight management, sexual health, hair health), clinician-led ongoing care, labs when required, prescription fulfillment, and subscription refills.

This spec is implementation-ready for a V1 that targets documented public behavior. Care-path protocols, state licensure, GLP-1/weight-loss eligibility, lab integrations, prescribing, pharmacy fulfillment, and subscription operations remain clinical/manual verification blockers.

## Goals

- Care-path intake per condition with clinical protocol.
- Clinician consult (async or video) and decision.
- Lab orders and results integration when protocols require.
- Prescription fulfillment via partner pharmacy with subscription refills.
- Ongoing care messaging with clinician.
- Transparent pricing and cancel-anytime.

## Non-Goals

- Do not provide medical advice outside a clinician.
- Do not copy Ro trademarks or marketing copy.
- Do not prescribe controlled substances without compliance review.
- Do not collect PHI beyond protocol needs.

## Detailed Design

### Source-Backed Product Requirements

- Condition-focused care paths with protocol-driven intake.
- Clinician consult (async messaging; video when protocol requires).
- Lab orders with partner labs; results ingestion.
- Prescription fulfillment and subscription refills with partner pharmacy.
- Continuous check-ins and progress logging.
- Shipment tracking, pause/cancel flows.
- Clear "not for emergencies" messaging.
- Care-path routing must separate weight management, sexual health, hair, fertility, skincare, labs, and any shop/non-Rx workflows.
- Intake must be protocol-versioned with eligibility criteria, contraindications, medication history, allergies, pregnancy/fertility considerations, photos/labs where required, and state support.
- Clinician review must support approve, decline, ask follow-up, request labs, require video, alternative plan, and urgent-care/emergency handoff.
- Lab orders must support partner location selection, order creation, specimen status, result ingestion, abnormal-result escalation, and downloadable summaries.
- Weight-loss flows must include BMI/comorbidity eligibility, GLP-1 insurance check, supply availability, dose cadence, side-effect check-ins, and clinician-only dose changes.
- Prescription fulfillment must support partner pharmacy, shipment tracking, refill cadence, pause/cancel, address-state checks, pharmacy rejection, and recall/adverse-event workflows.
- Progress tracking must support weight logs, photos, milestones, clinician review, export/delete, and privacy controls.
- Claims or insurance-assistance surfaces must distinguish benefit checks from guaranteed coverage.

## Core User Journeys

- User selects a care path, completes protocol-based intake.
- Clinician reviews; requests labs or issues decision.
- User completes labs at partner location; results auto-attached.
- Clinician issues Rx and plan; user subscribes to refills.
- User logs progress (for example, weight, photos) per cadence.
- User messages clinician within scope for dose adjustments.
- User pauses or cancels subscription.
- User updates address; shipping recalculated.
- User downloads lab and visit summaries.
- User deletes account and PHI.

## Data Model

- `User`: account, state, privacy, age flags.
- `CarePath`: id, category, protocol version.
- `IntakeResponse`: care-path id, answers, schema version.
- `ClinicianReview`: intake id, clinician id, decision, notes.
- `LabOrder`: lab partner id, tests, status.
- `LabResult`: values, ranges, attachments.
- `Prescription`: drug, dose, cadence, status.
- `Subscription` / `Shipment` / `Order`: fulfillment lifecycle.
- `ProgressEntry`: type, data, timestamps.
- `AuditEvent`: PHI access and consent events.

## Realtime, Push, And Offline Behavior

- Push covers intake status, clinician messages, labs ready, Rx fulfillment, shipments.
- Push payloads exclude lab values and drug details.
- Messaging near-real-time via WebSocket or push fallback.
- Offline: intake drafts, read-only results snapshots, shipment cache.
- Crisis resources cached for mental-health-adjacent flows where applicable.

## Permissions, Privacy, And Safety

- Camera and photo library only where intake or progress requires.
- Mic and camera only at video consult.
- Notifications at contextual points.
- "Not for emergencies" banners.
- PHI minimization; encrypted-at-rest; BAAs with clinical, pharmacy, and lab partners.
- No PHI in third-party analytics or ad SDKs.
- HIPAA-compliant posture with audit logging of PHI access.
- State-licensure routing per care path.
- Weight-loss medication eligibility rules (BMI, comorbidities) enforced by clinician.
- Controlled-substance flows gated behind compliance flag.
- Minor gating per care-path rules.
- Launch owners: clinical director, privacy/HIPAA counsel, compliance, security, fulfillment ops.

## Analytics And Monetization

- Privacy-safe events: care-path entry class, approval class, subscription lifecycle.
- Monetization via visit fees and subscription.
- No PHI-linked telemetry; ad SDKs excluded from clinical surfaces.

## Edge Cases

- Intake declined; refund/flow guidance.
- Lab result out of range; clinician follow-up required.
- Weight-loss medication eligibility not met; alternative plan.
- Controlled-substance request; compliance path.
- State unsupported for care path; block and guide.
- Shipment delayed causing dose gap; clinician notification.
- Minor user in adult-only care path.
- Progress photo privacy; explicit consent.
- Partner outage; queue and backoff.
- GLP-1 insurance checker returns unknown or denied; present self-pay/alternative path without guaranteeing coverage.
- Lab result is missing, delayed, or clinically abnormal; block next refill until clinician review where required.
- User becomes ineligible after weight/progress update; require clinician reassessment before shipment.
- Pharmacy or medication supply outage delays treatment; notify and route to clinician-managed alternatives.
- User moves to unsupported state while subscribed; pause shipment and re-run eligibility.
- Intake photo is poor quality or includes another person; reject or request recapture with deletion option.
- Emergency symptoms appear in intake or message; stop care-path flow and show immediate-care resources.
- Subscription cancellation overlaps pending lab or shipment; reconcile refund, retention, and clinical follow-up.

## Acceptance Criteria

- No PHI in third-party telemetry.
- Subscriptions are cancel-anytime and prorate policy is disclosed.
- Controlled-substance and weight-loss medication flows gated behind compliance flags.
- State-licensure routing correct per care path.
- Manual verification blockers resolved or feature-flagged.
- Exact source links are current or refreshed before implementation starts.
- Every care path has clinical protocol ownership, licensure policy, pharmacy/lab policy, adverse-event protocol, and privacy/compliance launch signoff.
- Weight-loss medication, lab, pharmacy, insurance-assistance, and subscription flows are launch-flagged until partner verification passes.

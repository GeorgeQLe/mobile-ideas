> Metadata

> - Category: Family location sharing and safety
> - Readiness status: Implementation-ready for a lawful public-source V1 clone as of 2026-05-01.
> - Verification basis: exact public marketplace pages, Life360 support articles, public product/service terms, privacy policy, and terms.
> - Manual verification blockers: live location accuracy, background-location prompts, battery impact, drive/crash detection, roadside/emergency dispatch partner handoff, Tile/Pet GPS-style tracker integration, subscription restore, and push/SOS payloads require test devices/accounts before one-for-one parity claims; safety lead, privacy lead, billing owner, partner-integration owner, and accessibility owner must keep them behind feature flags or launch-blocking acceptance tests.
> - Legal scope: functional parity only; do not use original code, brand, copy, iconography, illustrations, sample data, driving models, emergency workflows, or tracker integrations. Do not copy proprietary feature names, scoring models, or dispatch relationships.

## Overview

Build an original mobile family-locator app inspired by Life360: create a family circle, share real-time location, receive place-based arrival/departure alerts, view driving reports with events like hard braking and speeding, trigger SOS alerts, and optionally summon roadside assistance. Strong emphasis on mutual consent and anti-stalking safeguards.

## Goals

- Create "circles" (families/groups) and share real-time location with members.
- Define places (home, school, work) and alert on arrivals/departures.
- Offer driving summaries with originally computed events.
- Provide SOS alerts to circle members with one-tap dispatch metadata.
- Respect consent: all members can see who is tracking them and leave at any time.

## Non-Goals

- Do not enable covert tracking; device owner must be visible and consenting.
- Do not share data with advertisers or third-party analytics for location content.
- Do not copy proprietary driving-event ML or brand features.
- Do not operate as an emergency dispatch service beyond alerting circle members.

## Detailed Design

### Source-Backed Product Requirements

- Public listings verify circles/groups, advanced location sharing, location history, place alerts, SOS, crash detection, roadside assistance, driver reports, identity-protection membership benefits, and tracker-style item/pet integrations.
- Circle membership must require visible invitation acceptance; no account may be added silently or tracked without device-owner disclosure.
- Live location requires explicit foreground/background permission, clear battery disclosure, visible stale-location states, and per-circle sharing controls.
- Places/geofences must support arrival/departure notifications with rate limits and transparent member visibility.
- Driving and crash features must be original sensor/location heuristics, opt-in, confidence-scored, and framed as safety assistance, not guaranteed emergency detection.
- Drive detection must account for documented prerequisites: background app execution, sufficient battery, cell signal, minimum movement/speed thresholds, and motion/activity permission where available.
- SOS must send an alert to configured members/emergency contacts and clearly distinguish app alerts from public emergency dispatch.
- Roadside/emergency dispatch must be modeled as a licensed partner handoff with region availability, disclaimers, and audit logs.
- Tracker/item integration is optional in V1; any Tile/Pet GPS-equivalent support must use original compatible hardware abstractions or remain deferred.
- Identity-theft or data-breach benefits are out of scope unless a licensed partner integration and compliance review exist.
- Anti-abuse requirements are launch-critical: transparency list, leave-circle, location pause, member-added notices, and support escalation.
- Location history must be retention-limited, exportable, deletable, and unavailable to removed members after revocation.
- Child/teen members require parental consent/assent, age-appropriate notices, and no behavioral advertising based on precise location.

## Core User Journeys

- User installs, creates a circle, invites family.
- Invitee installs, accepts invite, grants location permission, sees circle map.
- User defines home and work places; arrivals trigger push to members.
- User takes a drive; trip detected; summary appears after arrival.
- User triggers SOS; members receive alert with last known location.
- User leaves a circle; location sharing to that circle stops immediately.
- User checks "who can see me" list and removes a member or leaves.
- User upgrades to premium; sees original entitlement copy.
- User disables location; circle shows "location off" state.
- User deletes account; all location history purged.
- User receives a member-added alert and reviews whether the new member can see them.
- User pauses location sharing for a circle and sees exactly how this appears to others.
- User configures crash/drive detection and sees why the app cannot detect every incident.
- User requests roadside assistance in a supported region and is handed to a licensed partner.
- User disables background location; map shows stale or limited state without shaming language.

## Data Model

- `User`: identity, phone/email, age gate, consent state.
- `Circle`: id, owner, members, invite codes.
- `Membership`: user, circle, role, joined-at, left-at.
- `LocationUpdate`: user, coordinates, accuracy, battery, timestamp, motion state.
- `Place`: circle, geofence shape/radius, name, members notified.
- `Alert`: type (arrival/departure/sos/driving), subject user, timestamp, details.
- `Trip`: user, start, end, path, events (original).
- `SOSEvent`: user, timestamp, location, status, acknowledged-by list.
- `RoadsideRequest`: user, handoff vendor id, status.
- `Entitlement`: plan, platform.
- `PrivacySettings`: retention window, analytics opt-in, crash/driving opt-in.
- `AuditEvent`: membership, location-share changes, SOS, privacy events.
- `LocationShareState`: user, circle, active/paused, reason, visible status.
- `EmergencyContact`: user, relationship, channels, verification state.
- `DispatchPartnerCase`: partner, region, request type, handoff status.
- `TrackerDevice`: owner, hardware id, type, last seen, battery, permission state.
- `ConsentRecord`: member, age band, location/driving/SOS consent versions.

## Realtime, Push, And Offline Behavior

- Location updates batched; upload frequency adapts to battery and motion.
- Websocket or push for real-time map and alerts.
- Offline queue for updates; replay on reconnect.
- Push notifications for alerts and SOS; SOS bypasses quiet hours with user consent.
- Background location requires explicit "Always" permission with clear disclosure.
- Crash/SOS alerts must use high-priority push only after explicit opt-in and must avoid precise coordinates in notification previews by default.
- Tracker hardware updates are deferred unless the downstream repo has lawful hardware/docs access.

## Permissions, Privacy, And Safety

- Precise location requires explicit "Always" permission; "While in use" fallback degrades gracefully.
- Anti-stalking: members see who tracks them and can leave unilaterally; no silent/covert mode.
- Minors require parental/guardian consent per region; COPPA-style review for under-13.
- Domestic-abuse threat model: cooldown before adding a new member, visible notification to existing members when someone joins, inability to hide from other members.
- Location history retention configurable and exportable; default minimal.
- No location data to advertisers.
- Driving-event analysis opt-in and revocable.
- Support tooling redacts location; raw access requires elevated approval.
- Launch owner: privacy lead, safety/trust lead, accessibility owner.
- Domestic-abuse threat model: provide leave-circle, location-pause, support contact, member-added notice, and "who can see me" affordances without notifying an abusive inviter before the user understands consequences.
- Do not provide stealth mode, hidden install, secret location history, or admin controls that suppress monitored-user notices.
- Raw precise-location access in support/admin tooling requires break-glass approval, reason logging, and automatic expiry.
- Regional legal review is required for minors, emergency dispatch, roadside assistance, crash detection, telematics, and identity-protection add-ons.

## Analytics And Monetization

- Track privacy-safe events: circle created, invite accepted, place created, SOS triggered, subscription events.
- No location content in analytics payloads.
- Free tier plus premium (extended history, roadside, more places); original paywall copy.
- Roadside dispatch handled by licensed partner; the app surfaces status only.
- Never sell precise location, driving events, or child location data for advertising. Any analytics must aggregate feature use and failure codes only.

## Edge Cases

- User denies background location; app runs in limited mode.
- Battery saver delays updates; explain staleness.
- Device offline in remote area; queue and replay.
- SOS triggered accidentally; 10-second cancel window.
- Circle owner leaves; ownership transfers to next admin.
- Minor added without explicit consent; block until verified.
- Spoofed location detection.
- Trip boundary errors (e.g., false starts).
- Roadside request in unsupported region.
- Account deletion with active SOS.
- Member tries to track a non-consenting adult or ex-partner.
- Crash detection false positive while phone is dropped.
- Crash detection false negative due to low battery, weak signal, or phone call.
- Location history requested by a removed member.
- Place alert fires repeatedly near a geofence boundary.
- Minor attempts to leave a family circle where parental supervision law varies by region.
- Roadside partner accepts request but later cancels.
- Tracker is paired to a shared item with disputed ownership.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Mutual-consent tracking enforced; no covert mode.
- Transparency list and leave-circle flow tested.
- SOS and roadside handoff functional with documented limitations.
- COPPA-style review for minors complete.
- Export and deletion accessible.
- Location sharing cannot be enabled without device-owner disclosure and revocable consent.
- Domestic-abuse and anti-stalking review passes before any background-location release.
- Crash, roadside, dispatch, identity, and tracker features remain off unless partner/legal/manual verification gates close.
- Manual verification blockers are either resolved with dated evidence or remain launch-blocking feature flags.

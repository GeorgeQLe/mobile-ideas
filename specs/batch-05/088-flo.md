# Flo-Style Clone Spec

> Metadata
> - Inspiration app: Flo
> - Category: period and ovulation tracking, fertility, pregnancy, perimenopause, symptoms, partner sharing, anonymous mode, subscriptions, and reproductive-health privacy
> - Readiness status: Implementation-ready for a lawful public-source V1 clone as of 2026-04-19.
> - Verification basis: exact public marketplace pages, official help/support pages, official privacy/legal pages, and public product documentation listed below.
> - Manual verification blockers: native iOS/Android screen capture, account/signup, age assurance, onboarding, cycle/symptom logging, prediction accuracy, pregnancy/perimenopause flows, Anonymous Mode switching, data export limits in Anonymous Mode, partner sharing, Secret Chats, wearable imports, Premium checkout/cancellation, notifications/reminders, deletion, support escalation, and regional medical/legal availability still require lawful test devices/accounts before one-for-one parity claims.
> - Legal scope: functional parity only; use original code, brand, copy, icons, media, datasets, models, workflows, contracts, pricing, recommendations, and support materials.

## Overview

Build an original cycle and reproductive-health tracker inspired by Flo's public workflow: onboard around cycle/pregnancy/fertility goals, log periods and symptoms, review predictions and insights, use partner sharing or anonymous mode where scoped, manage Premium, export/delete data, and protect reproductive-health privacy.

The clone must not copy marks, medical content, expert articles, prediction algorithms, Anonymous Mode implementation, Secret Chats content, partner-sharing workflows, Premium copy, pricing, support copy, and clinical review materials. Functional parity should use original or licensed content, synthetic test data, independently designed algorithms, platform-approved billing, and provider contracts approved for the downstream implementation.

This spec is implementation-ready for a V1 based on public sources. Any feature marked `Manual verification required` must remain behind a feature flag or launch blocker until lawful hands-on verification confirms native behavior and provider constraints.

## Goals

- Provide a mobile-first flo-inspired product with onboarding, core workflow, history/detail, settings, support, privacy, entitlement, and recovery flows.
- Preserve user trust expectations around privacy, safety, accessibility, subscriptions, notifications, data export, account deletion, and category-specific harm controls.
- Produce concrete screens, entities, API contracts, realtime/offline rules, analytics, edge cases, acceptance tests, and build phases for a downstream implementation repo.
- Keep public-source requirements, inferred clone requirements, and manual verification blockers visibly separate.
- Make every source-backed feature buildable with original assets and lawful integrations.

## Non-Goals

- Do not build a Flo-branded app or imply affiliation with Flo, its parent company, app stores, providers, creators, clinicians, partners, device makers, or payment processors.
- Do not copy proprietary content, datasets, ranking formulas, model outputs, recommendation systems, UI trade dress, screenshots, private APIs, pricing copy, support copy, or provider contracts.
- Do not scrape, reverse engineer, replay network traffic, bypass paid gates, bypass privacy controls, or use private platform entitlements.
- Do not treat sensitive education, wellness, fitness, health, location, reproductive-health, or wearable behavior as generic; each relevant surface needs an explicit owner and launch review.
- Do not claim exact App Store, Play Store, native-device, subscription, notification, support, deletion/export, device, account, regional, or provider parity until manual verification blockers are resolved.

## Detailed Design

### Source-Backed Product Requirements

- Public listings and Flo pages position the app around period, ovulation, fertility, pregnancy, symptom logging, personalized insights, partner sharing, Anonymous Mode, Secret Chats, wearables, and Premium.
- V1 must model signed-out preview, new member, returning member, anonymous-mode user, partner-linked user, trying-to-conceive user, pregnancy user, perimenopause user, Premium subscriber, expired subscriber, underage/age-limited user, restricted account, and deleted-account states.
- Onboarding must support age/region eligibility, cycle history, goals, consent for health data, privacy mode choice, reminder preference, accessibility preference, and medical disclaimers.
- Tracking must support period dates, flow, symptoms, mood, sex/contraception where scoped, discharge, basal temperature, ovulation tests, pregnancy milestones, perimenopause symptoms, and edit history.
- Predictions and insights must be explainable, uncertainty-aware, user-correctable, and explicitly not usable as birth control, diagnosis, treatment, or guaranteed conception support.
- Anonymous Mode must create separate account state, remove personal identifiers from health data, limit unsupported features, and surface export/support limitations before switch.
- Partner sharing and Secret Chats require explicit opt-in, content moderation, consent boundaries, block/report, age controls, and incompatibility handling with Anonymous Mode.
- Settings must expose privacy mode, data export/delete, health imports, reminders, partner links, Premium, support, terms, privacy, and consent withdrawal.

### Manual Verification Required

- native iOS/Android screen capture, account/signup, age assurance, onboarding, cycle/symptom logging, prediction accuracy, pregnancy/perimenopause flows, Anonymous Mode switching, data export limits in Anonymous Mode, partner sharing, Secret Chats, wearable imports, Premium checkout/cancellation, notifications/reminders, deletion, support escalation, and regional medical/legal availability.
- Exact native navigation order, copy, permission timing, loading/error states, platform billing behavior, support outcomes, notification payloads, and regional differences.

## Core User Journeys

- New user installs the app, reviews an original value proposition, completes eligibility and preference setup, and reaches the default Flo-style home surface without unsupported permissions.
- Returning user resumes the most recent meaningful activity, sees stale/offline state clearly, completes the core action, and confirms synced server state after reconnect.
- User denies a requested permission, receives a usable fallback, and can later re-enable that permission from settings without losing local work.
- User starts a paid trial or subscription, loses entitlement through cancellation/refund/expiration, and sees consistent locked/unlocked state across devices.
- Privacy-focused user changes notification/privacy settings, requests data export, deletes the account, and sees consequences for retained records before confirming.
- Support-seeking user reports a product, billing, privacy, or safety issue with redacted evidence and receives a trackable support case.

## API And Backend Contracts

- `POST /auth/session`, `POST /auth/anonymous`, `POST /auth/recover`, `DELETE /auth/session`: account and Anonymous Mode auth with age/region state.
- `PATCH /health-profile`, `POST /consents`, `DELETE /consents/:id`: health goals, consent, privacy mode, and age eligibility.
- `GET /cycle/dashboard`, `GET /cycles`, `POST /cycles`, `PATCH /cycles/:id`: cycle timeline, predictions, edit history, and uncertainty labels.
- `POST /period-entries`, `POST /symptom-entries`, `POST /fertility-signals`: sensitive health logging with validation, deletion/export eligibility, and audit.
- `GET /predictions`, `POST /predictions/:id/feedback`: ovulation/period/pregnancy predictions, explainability, corrections, and model versioning.
- `GET /insights`, `GET /content/:id`: educational content, medical disclaimers, age controls, language/region availability, and entitlement gates.
- `POST /anonymous-mode/switch`, `POST /anonymous-mode/disable`: account transition, data transfer limits, unsupported feature warnings, and irreversible states.
- `POST /partner-links`, `DELETE /partner-links/:id`: partner sharing, consent, revoked access, and Anonymous Mode incompatibility.
- `POST /community/posts`, `POST /community/reports`, `POST /users/:id/block`: Secret Chats/community moderation where scoped.
- `GET /entitlements`, `POST /checkout/session`, `POST /billing/restore`, `POST /billing/webhook`: Premium entitlement verification.
- `POST /data-export`, `DELETE /account`, `POST /support/cases`: data rights, Anonymous Mode limitations, deletion, and support workflows.

## Realtime, Push, And Offline Behavior

- Cache cycle dashboard, predictions, history, settings, reminders, consent state, and entitlement snapshot with model-version freshness labels.
- Allow local logging and history edits offline; block account switching, partner links, community, checkout, export, deletion, and support evidence upload.
- Sensitive entries must sync idempotently, preserve edit history, and avoid analytics emission until consent and privacy mode are confirmed.
- Predictions must be labeled stale when local edits are unsynced or model versions change after reconnect.
- Push payloads must avoid explicit period, pregnancy, sex, fertility, symptom, partner, community, or subscription-sensitive details.
- Anonymous Mode must keep local identifiers separate, prevent unsupported cloud support flows, and warn before irreversible account replacement.

## Permissions, Privacy, And Safety

- Reproductive-health data, Anonymous Mode, partner sharing, pregnancy/fertility claims, minors/age controls, community content, wearable imports, and subscriptions are launch blockers with named owners.
- Do not claim contraception, diagnosis, treatment, guaranteed ovulation, fertility success, pregnancy outcome, or emergency advice; route concerning symptoms to reviewed medical guidance.
- Privacy mode requires identifier separation, no health-data ad sharing, support limitations, export limitations, and clear irreversible-transition copy.
- Partner sharing must be explicit, revocable, granular, and never default-on; shared data must be visible before sending.
- Community and sexual-health content require age controls, moderation, report/block, crisis/harm escalation, and region-sensitive content review.
- Accessibility must cover calendar alternatives, screen-reader chart summaries, dynamic type, private-mode affordances, color-independent cycle states, and sensitive-notification controls.

## Analytics And Monetization

- Track privacy-safe events: goal_setup_completed, cycle_entry_logged, prediction_viewed, insight_opened, anonymous_mode_started, partner_link_created, premium_paywall_viewed, data_export_requested.
- Every event must use opaque object ids, version ids, result buckets, latency buckets, entitlement state, locale, and error codes rather than raw user content or sensitive health/location/payment data.
- Separate product telemetry from safety, billing, support, and privacy audit records so deletion/export policies can be applied correctly.
- Do not send raw images, precise location, exact health entries, therapy/care details, private notes, food diaries, reproductive-health details, payment credentials, or support evidence as analytics properties.
- Monetization may include original subscriptions, trials, sponsored benefits, or licensed bundles only after legal, privacy, payment, and platform review.
- Any paid feature must disclose price, trial, renewal, cancellation path, refund/support owner, regional availability, and data-sharing implications before launch.
